Token Overview

ROOT5 exists as two separate token systems serving different purposes within the ecosystem. Bridge Mechanics

ROOT5 uses different bridging mechanisms depending on the ecosystem. Understanding how tokens move between chains is crucial to address concerns about "minting" functions.

EVM ‚Üî EVM Bridges (Currently Live)

How it works: LayerZero V2 Omnichain Fungible Token (OFT) standard

1. User Initiates
Send 100 ROOT5 from BSC to Ethereum
‚Üí
2. Burn on Source
100 ROOT5 burned on BSC
‚Üí
3. DVN Verification
Multiple verifiers confirm burn
‚Üí
4. Mint on Destination
100 ROOT5 minted on Ethereum

Why minting is secure:

  • Tokens can ONLY be minted when an equal amount is burned on another chain
  • Multiple independent DVNs (Decentralized Verifier Networks) verify every burn
  • Total supply across ALL chains always remains 500M
  • No single entity can mint without burning - it's mathematically enforced
  • LayerZero V2 is battle-tested with billions in TVL

Cost: $1-10 per bridge depending on destination chain gas fees

Solana ‚Üî EVM Bridge (V1 - Centralized Swap with Burn)

Status: In development - planned for Q1-Q2 2025

V1 Approach: Centralized swap system for easy supply control and security. Burns 50% of Solana deposits while maintaining 1:1 swap ratio to EVM. Future V2 may be fully decentralized.

Centralized Swap Mechanism (V1):

  • Centralized Control: Trusted swap operated by ROOT5 DAO team
  • Solana ‚Üí EVM: Burn 50% of deposited Solana tokens, swap remaining 1:1 to EVM
  • EVM ‚Üí Solana: Standard 1:1 swap from EVM pool until depleted
  • Automatic Processing: Deposits processed automatically within 24 hours
  • Supply Management: Centralized control prevents abuse and ensures proper burning
  • Transparency: All burns publicly verifiable on Solana explorer
  • Multi-sig Security: Team multi-signature wallet controls swap operations
Solana ‚Üí EVM Example:
1. User deposits 10,000 Solana ROOT5 to swap address
2. System burns 5,000 Solana ROOT5 (50% deflationary burn)
3. Remaining 5,000 held in reserve pool
4. User receives 10,000 EVM ROOT5 on chosen chain (1:1 swap rate)
5. Net effect: -5K Solana supply, +10K EVM supply, total supply reduced by 5K
EVM ‚Üí Solana Example:
1. User deposits 10,000 EVM ROOT5 to swap address
2. EVM tokens held in reserve pool
3. User receives 10,000 Solana ROOT5 from reserve (1:1 swap rate)
4. Limitation: Only available while Solana reserve pool has tokens
5. Net effect: Reserve pool gradually depletes as more users swap to Solana

Why Centralized V1:

  • Faster development and lower cost than fully decentralized solution
  • Easy supply control and burn verification
  • Prevents smart contract risks during initial phase
  • Can adjust parameters based on community feedback
  • Team accountability and transparent operations
  • Future V2 can migrate to decentralized bridge if needed

Estimated Development Cost:

  • Solana PDA/smart contract: ~7 SOL ($1,400 at $200/SOL)
  • EVM smart contract development: $3,000-5,000
  • Backend automation system: $2,000-3,000
  • Security review: $2,000-5,000
  • Total estimated: $8,400-14,400

Swap Processing Fee: $2-5 per transaction (covers gas + operations, fee burned)

Configuration Cost (when available): ~$50-100 in gas fees

Bridge Fee (estimated): $0.50-2 per transaction (TON has very low fees)

Why Bridge Mechanisms Matter

Many people see "mint" functions in smart contracts and assume it means unlimited inflation. Here's why ROOT5's minting is different:

  • Controlled Minting: Tokens can ONLY be minted when equal amounts are burned elsewhere
  • Verified Burning: Multiple independent parties (DVNs) must verify burns before minting
  • Mathematical Guarantee: Total supply (1.5B) is mathematically enforced across all chains
  • No Owner Privilege: Even the contract owner cannot mint without a corresponding burn
  • Transparent: All bridge transactions visible on LayerZero Scan and chain explorers
For Skeptics: You can verify that total supply remains constant by checking all contract addresses on their respective explorers. The sum will always equal 1.5 billion tokens (1B Solana + 500M EVM).
